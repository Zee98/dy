About Dynamic Youth

We are a volunteer, youth-led and non-for-profit NGO working on the localization of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) in Afghanistan!

Featured Programs

Programs that we offer currently.

Career & Education

Skills development training increase Afghan youth prospects through engaging, innovative and diverse training projects. Our Career and Education program is designed to offer jobs related skills, professional development and training opportunities to inspire and create some grounds for learning, sharing and growth. We aim to generate entrepreneurs and community leaders.

Peace Building

Our Peace Building initiative aims to strengthen the capacity of Afghan youth in conflict transformation and provide an opportunity for them to learn and implement peace education (the process of acquiring the values, knowledge and developing the attitudes, skills and behaviors to live in harmony with oneself, with others, and with the natural environment. We want Afghans to advocate peace in their local communities to counter extreme violent, hate and every kind of discrimination.

Leadership Development

Leadership motivates the people to a higher level of performance through their strong human relations. It is an important function of management which helps to maximize efficiency and to achieve organizational goals, so managers must have traits of a leader. Through our leadership Development Program, we train and develop a set of skills required for a servant leader in students and this program guides Afghan youth to be the leader they would willingly follow. Our priority is to teach them lead themselves.

Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) is the largest global partnership agreement and development plan for the planet ever made. It was agreed upon by all United Nations Member state in September 2015. and consists of 17 goals and 169 targets. The goals ultimately aspire to create a better, more inclusive and more prosperous world by 2030.

Women Empowerment

Women and youth are the majority population in Afghanistan. With a staggering 63% of the population being under the age of 25, Afghanistan holds one of the largest percentages of youth populations in the world. Due to their large number, youth have become a target for extremist organization recruitment efforts.We need to develop their skills and work on a roadmap to promote inclusive and meaningful participation of youth specially women in different sectors, most of all leadership positions, decision making levels and civic engagements.
